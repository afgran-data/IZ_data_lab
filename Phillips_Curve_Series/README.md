# Phillips Curve Series Analysis

## Project Series

### 1. Headline CPI vs. Unemployment

**Objective**

Analyze the historical relationship between headline Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation and the U.S. unemployment rate.

**Dataset**

- CPIAUCSL – Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers
- UNRATE – Civilian Unemployment Rate
- USREC – U.S. Recession Indicator
- 
---

### 2. Core CPI vs. Unemployment

**Objective**

Examine whether Core CPI (excluding food and energy) exhibits a stronger or more stable relationship with unemployment than headline inflation.

**Dataset**

- CPILFESL – Core Consumer Price Index
- UNRATE – Civilian Unemployment Rate
- USREC – U.S. Recession Indicator

---

### 3. Core PCE vs. Unemployment

**Objective**

Analyze the relationship between Core Personal Consumption Expenditures (Core PCE) inflation and unemployment.

Core PCE is the Federal Reserve's preferred measure of inflation because it better captures changes in consumer spending behavior and excludes volatile food and energy prices.

**Dataset**

- PCEPILFE – Core Personal Consumption Expenditures Price Index
- UNRATE – Civilian Unemployment Rate
- USREC – U.S. Recession Indicator

---

## Purpose

The goal of this project is to build an intuitive understanding of the Phillips Curve by comparing different measures of inflation with unemployment over several decades. The analyses emphasize historical interpretation and visualization rather than forecasting, helping illustrate how inflation and labor market relationships have changed across different economic environments.
